FFIEC CDR · via FDIC BankFind

Every FDIC-insured institution files a quarterly Report of Condition & Income (call report). Of the 56 tracked applications, eight involve insured institutions with a Q1 2026 call report on file — the newly chartered Erebor Bank plus seven banks that hold charters from earlier approvals or conversions. Uninsured national trust banks (Circle, Ripple, etc.) are not FDIC-insured and generally do not appear in the public FFIEC CDR database; the OCC reports them separately on its active trust-bank list.

Key facts from the data

Sourced & dated

01 · VOLUME

18 filings in 2025 = prior four years combined

Eighteen de novo charter applications were filed with the OCC in 2025 (Banking Dive), matching the total for 2021–2024. Eighteen more were filed in H1 2026. From 2011–2024 the OCC averaged fewer than four charter applications per year (Comptroller Gould).

02 · SPEED

Conditional approvals now run ~4 months

Since September 2025, the median time from filing to conditional approval is roughly 120–130 days (VALT 120, Nubank 121, Mercury and Erebor 126, Morgan Stanley 120). Varo's 2018 preliminary approval took ~430 days; Coinbase (181) and Sony's Connectia (269) are the recent outliers.

03 · MIX

Trust charters outnumber full-service 2-to-1

Of 56 tracked applications: 23 are de novo national trust banks (uninsured, custody/fiduciary — no deposit-taking), 12 full-service de novo banks, 11 conversions, 2 other digital-asset licensing filings, and 8 pre-2024 applications (3 now operating, 3 withdrawn, 2 lapsed).

04 · CALL REPORTS

8 filers hold $194.7B in combined assets

Only insured, operating banks file FFIEC call reports. The eight Q1 2026 filers in this dataset hold $194.7B of assets — $122.3B (63%) at UBS Bank USA alone. Erebor, the only newly-opened de novo, shows $1.70B of assets and zero loans seven weeks after opening.

05 · PIPELINE

20 approved-and-waiting, 18 still in queue

Twenty applicants hold conditional approvals and must still raise capital, pass pre-opening exams, and (for full-service banks) win FDIC insurance before opening. Eighteen applications remain undecided — Wise's is the oldest, pending 397 days as of Jul 19, 2026.

06 · PRECEDENT

Two earlier conditional approvals expired unused

Paxos (CA April 2021) and Protego (CA February 2021) never opened within their approval windows and their approvals lapsed. Both refiled in 2025 and were re-approved on December 12, 2025 — illustrating that a conditional approval is not a charter.

Methodology, definitions & sources

  • Scope. Novel charter activity filed with the OCC July 2019 – July 2026: de novo full-service national banks, de novo national trust banks, state/thrift→national conversions, CEBA credit-card banks, and digital-asset licensing applications, plus the notable pre-2024 fintech applications (Varo, SoFi, Agility, Anchorage, Paxos, Protego, Figure, Oportun). Routine mergers, branches and holding-company reorganizations are excluded.
  • Approval stages. "Preliminary approval" and "conditional approval" are the same stage: the OCC's preliminary conditional approval lets organizers raise capital and prepare the bank, but does not allow it to open. Final approval (charter issuance) follows completion of conditions and, for insured banks, FDIC insurance. Conditional approvals typically expire in 12–18 months if unused.
  • Dates. Filing dates are from OCC control numbers, decision letters, and the OCC digital-assets docket. Where the OCC has not published a receipt date, the month is estimated from control-number sequences and marked ~ / †.
  • People. Names are transcribed from the "Organizers, Directors and Officers" tables in OCC decision letters and the public volumes of applications. Names link to LinkedIn people-search results (direct profile URLs are not independently verified).
  • Call-report data. FFIEC CDR / FDIC BankFind, quarter ended 3/31/2026, retrieved July 19, 2026. Uninsured national trust banks generally do not appear in the public CDR database; the OCC reports them on its active trust-bank list.
  • Not investment or legal advice. Unofficial tracker compiled from public sources; statuses change without notice — verify against OCC records.
